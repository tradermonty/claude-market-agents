FOMC — April 29, 2026: Fed Holds Rates Steady at 3.50%–3.75% (8-4 Vote)
In what is expected to be Jerome Powell's final FOMC press conference as Chair, the Federal Reserve voted 8-4 to keep the federal funds rate unchanged at 3.50%–3.75%. The four-member dissent — a level not seen since October 1992 — reflected deep internal disagreement: Gov. Miran voted for a 25bp cut, while Hammack, Kashkari, and Logan dissented against including an "easing bias" in the statement. Powell signaled he will remain on the Board of Governors pending resolution of a Federal Reserve renovation transparency investigation. Meanwhile, geopolitical tensions — including a reported U.S. naval blockade of Iranian ports — drove WTI crude +7% on the day, adding inflationary complexity to the Fed's deliberations.

Today's Key Points
FOMC
Fed's Historic Split Decision: The 8-4 hold vote — with four-way dissent unseen since 1992 — signals the FOMC is deeply divided between inflation hawks and growth doves. Powell's ambiguity about remaining on the Board adds governance uncertainty. Markets must now price both an "easing bias" statement and a potential hawkish pivot by incoming Chair Warsh.
OIL
Oil Shock Reshapes the Macro Tape: WTI surging +7% to ~$107 and Brent breaching $114 injects a stagflation risk premium. Energy (+1.99%) was the sole clear sector winner. Rate-sensitive sectors — Utilities (-1.46%), Real Estate (-0.84%), Basic Materials (-1.64%) — bore the pain. Watch $110 WTI as the next key level; a sustained break could force a Fed rethink.
SEMI
Semiconductor Earnings Wave Dominates: SIMO (+46%), EXTR (+28%), NXPI (+26%), INTC (+12%) single-handedly kept NASDAQ in the green while the Dow fell 280 points. The breadth of semiconductor beats — across RFID chips (PI), network semiconductors (NXPI), test equipment (FORM), and x86 CPUs (INTC) — suggests a broad AI-driven hardware upcycle rather than isolated beats.
EARN
Strong After-Hours Earnings Parade: 15 stocks rose 2%+ after the close on earnings — led by $PI +23.71%, $FORM +17.10%, $VIAV +12.22%, $CVNA +8.60% (EPS +270%). The average EPS surprise was +23.2%. Notably, $CVNA and $BE both delivered massive EPS beats, suggesting corporate margins are holding up despite macro headwinds from oil and rates.
GOLD
Gold Falls Despite Geopolitical Tension: Gold spot fell to $4,566 (-0.92%) in an unusual divergence from rising geopolitical risk. The driver appears to be rising real yields and a firmer dollar as oil-driven inflation expectations keep rate cuts at bay. This is consistent with a stagflationary regime rather than pure risk-off — a context where neither bonds nor gold reliably hedge equity drawdowns.
BOND
Long End Under Pressure — 30-Year Near 5%: TLT fell -0.78%, and the 30-year yield approaches 5% — a psychologically significant level that historically triggers asset allocation shifts. The combination of an FOMC easing bias in the statement but inflation fears from oil creates a steepening risk in the yield curve. Duration-heavy portfolios face mounting headwinds.
BRDTH
Narrow Market Leadership Warning: Only 686 stocks in confirmed uptrends against 81 volume-surge names. The QQQ's fractional gain was entirely driven by a handful of semiconductor names. Small-cap IWM -0.67%, Dow -0.57% — the divergence between mega-cap tech and the broader market is widening. A reversal in semiconductors could expose significant index vulnerability.
