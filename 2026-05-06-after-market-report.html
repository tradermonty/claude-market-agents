📋 Executive Summary

U.S. equities delivered a broad-based rally on Wednesday, May 6, 2026, with all four major indices closing sharply higher as a confluence of bullish catalysts lifted sentiment. The S&P 500 gained +1.48% to close at 7,366.46 — a fresh intraday record high — while the NASDAQ Composite surged +2.05% to 25,846.21 on the back of a powerful semiconductor rally. The Dow Jones Industrial Average added +1.24% to 49,910.58, and the Russell 2000 small-cap index climbed +1.47% to 2,886.77.


The session was dominated by three catalysts: (1) AMD's blow-out Q1 2026 earnings (reported Tuesday after close, revenue +38% YoY) ignited a global chip rally that lifted AMD +15%, Intel +4.5%, ARM +13.6%, and NVDA +5.7%; (2) Super Micro Computer (SMCI) surged +24.5% after strong Q3 results featuring margin recovery and a bullish AI server revenue forecast; and (3) rising optimism over a potential U.S.-Iran peace deal which sent crude oil sharply lower (−7.6% to $101.52/bbl Brent) while gold climbed +3.0% to $430.90 on safe-haven demand and dollar softness. After the close, Fortinet (FTNT) surged +18.4% in after-hours on strong Q1 beats, while AppLovin (APP) rose +7.2% and Arm Holdings (ARM) added another +5.1%. DoorDash (DASH) jumped +10.4% despite a slight earnings miss, rewarded for stronger-than-expected revenue guidance.
